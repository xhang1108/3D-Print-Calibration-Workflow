Retraction Calibration

What is Retraction?

Retraction is the process of pulling back the filament slightly to prevent oozing and stringing during non-printing moves. Proper retraction settings can significantly improve print quality.

Calibration Process

  1. Start with your slicer's default retraction settings
  2. Print a retraction test model (e.g., retraction towers or cubes)
  3. Adjust retraction distance and speed
  4. Repeat the test print with new settings
  5. Fine-tune until you achieve minimal stringing and oozing

Factors to Consider

  • Filament type (e.g., PLA vs. PETG)
  • Direct drive vs. Bowden extruder
  • Nozzle temperature
  • Print speed
  • Travel move length

Tips

  • Start with lower retraction distances for direct drive extruders
  • Bowden setups typically require longer retraction distances
  • Consider enabling coasting or wiping in your slicer
  • Adjust retraction settings for each filament type
